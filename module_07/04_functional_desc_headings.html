Functional and Descriptive Headings

The headings you use to organize information in your outline can be used to help structure your report.

While the structure remains relatively the same for all workplace documents, as workplace documents present more complex and detailed information, you must pay greater attention to structuring that information in a way that’s easy for your readers to follow. Headings and sub-headings assist readers by making them easy to review (skim) and find information.

Use functional headings at the start and end of reports to help readers determine where they are in the report, and descriptive headings in the middle to help readers figure out what the report is about.

Headings for report are generally divided into 2 categories:

  1. Functional headings generally guide the reader into and out of the report by indicating the placement of the information in the report (e.g. Purpose, Background, Methodology, Discussion, Conclusions, Recommendations)
  2. Descriptive headings are used to accurately describe the information that is being presented (e.g. Reasons for Delay, Printing Needs)
