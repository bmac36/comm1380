Presenting Information and Social Media

Week 7 Introduction

This week we’ll cover the process of presenting information in a workplace document. To model this process, you will be asked to compose an informal report in the form of a short memo for your employer.

The activities and lecture content is designed to familiarize you with the steps we take when we compose an informal report. We begin by identifying the importance of planning and structuring your information for clarity by using different types of headings and subheadings. We then differentiate between conclusions and recommendations. We’ll cover how to conduct and present primary research. We’ll also provide you with a short introduction to different types of social media used in business. This will help prepare you for the Social Media Project.

Learning Outcomes

After you complete this week's learning, you should know how to:

  • plan, write and edit business reports or proposals for specific audiences, purposes and situations, applying appropriate style, tone and format;
  • discuss the types of research used in business;
  • select appropriate research for the audience and purpose of a business report;
  • integrate research into a report by summarizing, paraphrasing or quoting research sources;
  • correctly document research using the assigned format; and
  • identify different types of social media used in business, and explain the appropriat application of each.
