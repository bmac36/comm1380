Presenting Information and Social Media

Week 7 Summary

This week, we covered the process we go through when we present information in informal reports.

Those steps are:

  • Step 1: Plan and outline
  • Step 2: Collect and analyse information
  • Step 3: Revise headings and compose
  • Step 4: Conclusion or recommendations

Chunking content through the use of headings and subheadings helps you pivot from outline to composition. Headings also make it much easier for your readers to parse your information. We also covered how to present primary research. What’s important here is showing that you’ve done the work to ensure that your report is credible to your readers while also demonstrating that you understand what your readers need to understand from your research.

We concluded with an overview of some business applications of social media. Can you remember the different platforms that fit into the five general categories of social media, and how they might be used by businesses?

Here’s a list of the five categories:

  • Blogs
  • Micro-blogs
  • Podcasts
  • Social Networking Sites
  • Photo- and Video-Sharing Sites

The assignment this week is intended to pull all of these lecture materials and activities together and help prepare you for future activities.
