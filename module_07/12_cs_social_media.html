Presenting Information and Social Media

Case Study: Social Media Project

For the Social Media Project, you’re putting into practice everything we’ve covered this week. You must compose an informal report for your boss about social media using primary and secondary research.

Scenario

You are working in an office and your boss, Carlton Pierre, has called you into his office. A coworker just forwarded him article about social media and business called “How do you use social media to meet your business goals?” Carlton admits to you that he doesn’t know much about social media, or your organization’s use of it. He’d like to learn more and help ensure that your company’s social media strategy is effective but doesn’t have much time. He hands you the article and says, "Here. Read this. Let me know what it says and have a look at how we’re using social media. Tell me how we are currently using Social Media and is there anything we could be doing better based on this article.".


Tips

You are not required to search for and find information about social media from secondary sources (although you can); you are being given that information in the form of an article.

The article provides some real life best practices and examples of how social media is currently being used by different businesses.

The focus of the Social Media Project is to verify the key facts from this article and determine, through your own primary research, whether they apply to the situation you are trying to address, which in this case is the social media efforts of the company of your choice.

The assignment allows you to choose which organization's social media presence you would like to analyse.

Instructions

  1. Read the Social Media Article .
  2. Select a business, government agency (such as a municipality or government ministry), or an NGO that uses at least 3 social media applications as the organization for this scenario and whose social media presence you would be interested in studying.
  3. Write an informal memo report to your boss using the Sample Outline .
    • Summarize or paraphrase the key points form the article regarding key goals for Social Media.
    • Because the boss doesn’t know much about social media, explain what each social media apps is before describing how it is being used by the organization.
    • Using the key points from the article points as the basis for analysis, review at least 3 social media applications that the organization uses.
    • Make any suggestions for improvements, if appropriate.
    • The outline template is provided to help you get started with the Social Media Project and help you understand how brainstorming and outlining can help you transition facts, data and information into workplace documents.
    • Use the template as a guide
      • do not view these as the required headings
      • do not use these generic headings in your final document
      • use the appropriate structure, and functional and descriptive headings, that suits the content in your final document
  4. Submit your completed document to the Social Media Project submission folder by the due date indicated on the Instructional Plan.

Requirements

  1. Use report structure and design strategies to organize your findings into the appropriate headings and sub-headings each part of the report.
  2. Use 12 point Calibri or Arial font and 1.15 spacing for the text.
  3. Use block format, which has paragraphs flush left with a space between paragraphs and headings.
